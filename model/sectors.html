Sectors¶

The main role of sectors is to match the future demand coming from the market-clearing algorithm with each agent, so that each agent can decide how to operate the assets available and invest in new ones if needed. A secondary role is match the agents’ assets to the forward demand trajectory, in order to compute the consumption, production, and the cost of supply a commodity. Consumption and production of energy commodities are passed back to the market-clearing algorithm; the cost of supply of a commodity is calculated and passed only by conversion and supply sectors and it is used by the market-clearing algorithm to update the commodity prices when it operates in the “equilibrium” or “budget” mode.

Inputs¶

The input market from the MCA. In general, it is the result of running sectors with higher priorities. It contains three components:

  • Demand (consumption), \(\mathcal{D}_{c, s, r}\), commodities consumed by sectors

  • Production (supply), \(\mathcal{C}_{c, s, r}\), commodities produced by sectors

  • Prices, \(\mathcal{I}_{c, s, r}\), current market prices

2. Agent-agent interactions¶

Performs agent-agent interactions. Canonically, MUSE agents interact in pairs where one agent handles investment into _new_ demand, whereas the other handles retrofitting. In that instance, agents in pair interact by exchanging assets (from new to retrofit agents) and information about market makeup (from retrofit to new agents).

  • the interactions are called in sectors.Sector.next()

    245
    self.interactions(self.agents)

  • the inputs consists only of the list of all agents in a sector

  • the agents are likely to be modified in-place by the call

  • the interaction function above is created from the TOML inputs via interactions.factory(), called in sectors.Sector.factory()

  • interactions are defined in interactions

  • register_interaction_net() registers new ways to figure out Groups of interacting agents

  • register_agent_interaction() registers new interactions proper

3. Agent investment¶

Agents are allowed to invest in order to service the demand for the forecast year. In practice, there are two parts to the investment process:

  1. The demand is split according to a user-defined function into seperate blocks to be serviced by each agent

  2. A loop over the agents calls for each agent to invest the demand determined above

The method in the first step above is any method registered with @register_demand_share. In practice, their is currently only one such method, new_and_retro(). In this particular setup, agents come in pairs. Broadly, a pair services a share of the demand which corresponds to the assets the pair owns. However, the new agent services only the deman corresponding to an increase in consumption, whereas the retrofit agent services the demand which corresponds to its newly decommisioned assets. A more detailled description can be found here.

The computation is called in in sectors.Sector.next(), via sectors.Sector.investment()

Outputs¶

A market containing the production, consumption and cost of supplying commodities specific to this market. It is defined as follows:

  • Production (supply), \(\mathcal{C}_{c, s, r}\), commodities produced by the sector

  • Demand (consumption), \(\mathcal{D}_{c, s, r}\), commodities consumed by the sector

  • Costs associated with the consumption, \(\mathcal{O}_{c, s, r}\)

  • A coordinate comm_usage defining each commodity according to its usage (see muse.commodities). More specifically, it allows the caller (the MCA) to know which commodity is produced by the sector (see muse.mca.single_year_iteration()).
